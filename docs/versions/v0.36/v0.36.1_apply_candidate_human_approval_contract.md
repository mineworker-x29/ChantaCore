# v0.36.1 Apply Candidate & Human Approval Contract

Parent track: Human-approved Patch Apply Sandbox.

v0.36.1 creates apply candidate and human approval contract metadata only. It builds on v0.36.0 Patch Apply Sandbox Boundary and v0.35.9 Controlled Patch Proposal Layer v1. It may consume v0.35.6 Human Review Packet metadata, v0.35.5 Patch Risk Scanner metadata, and v0.35.4 Diff Proposal Envelope metadata, but it does not treat those artifacts as apply permission.

## Definitions

ApplyCandidateEnvelope is a bounded metadata envelope that binds proposed review/risk/diff artifacts to a human approval contract and an eligibility decision. An apply candidate is not patch application.

HumanApprovalContract records the required operator-supplied approval evidence for future dry-run eligibility. A human approval contract is not apply execution.

HumanApprovalEvidence records bounded evidence such as explicit operator confirmation, reviewer identity reference, approval timestamp, candidate reference, diff reference, scope reference, approval statement, or ticket reference. Approval evidence is not filesystem authority.

ApplyEligibilityDecision may classify a candidate as eligible_for_future_dry_run, blocked, review_required, future_gated, or no_op. Future dry-run eligibility is not dry-run execution.

## Approval Rules

Only operator_supplied_explicit_approval, operator_supplied_review_record, and operator_supplied_cli_metadata can validate future dry-run eligibility. Test fixtures may represent operator evidence in tests only.

v0.35 review metadata is not v0.36 apply approval. Human review packet metadata alone, approval gate metadata, and review decision records are review metadata only.

Model-generated approval is invalid. Automated placeholder approval is invalid. Inferred approval is invalid. Missing approval is invalid. No approval evidence grants patch application.

## Bounded Agentic Task Policy

v0.36 agentic operation means bounded function/task execution metadata and future-gated single-cycle operation. It is not independent autonomous agent runtime, not a multi-cycle loop, not external agent orchestration, and not Dominion runtime. v0.36.1 may preserve bounded agentic task metadata only; no task cycle executes in this release.

## Taxonomies

Candidate kind taxonomy includes from_review_packet, from_diff_proposal, from_structured_patch, from_unified_diff, from_risk_accepted_review, from_manual_operator_request, blocked_candidate, no_op_candidate, and unknown.

Source taxonomy includes v0.36.0 apply sandbox boundary, v0.35.9 handoff packet and consolidation report, v0.35.8 CLI surface, v0.35.7 trace packet, v0.35.6 review packet/approval metadata/decision record, v0.35.5 risk report, v0.35.4 diff/structured/unified proposals, v0.35.3 patch plan, manual operator input, test fixture, and unknown.

Status and readiness taxonomy distinguish candidate_created, candidate_validated, human_approval_attached, human_approval_validated, eligible_for_future_dry_run, blocked, review_required, future_gated, no_op, candidate_contract_ready, human_approval_contract_ready, approval_evidence_validated, eligibility_decision_ready, and v0.36.2/v0.36.3 design handoff.

Decision and risk taxonomy distinguish metadata allowances from deny/block/review/future-gate outcomes and risk kinds such as missing review/diff/risk report, blocked risk report, missing or invalid human approval, model-generated approval, review metadata treated as apply approval, stale approval, scope/diff mismatch, patch apply, live write, shell/test/install, external agent, Dominion, and infinite loop risks.

Approval source/evidence taxonomy separates operator-supplied evidence from human review packet metadata, model-generated approval, automated placeholders, inferred approval, and missing approval.

Human approval validation taxonomy includes operator source checks, model-generated rejection, review-metadata-not-apply checks, candidate/diff/scope binding, timestamp, ambiguity, stale approval, and no-apply-permission checks.
